# Stone & Heen Feedback Triggers -- Citations

## Primary anchor

1. **Stone, D., & Heen, S. (2014)** *Thanks for the Feedback: The
   Science and Art of Receiving Feedback Well.* Penguin.
   Three-trigger framework (truth, relationship, identity) and the
   intervention catalog (acknowledge-first, separate-data-from-source,
   recast-identity).

## Supporting anchors

2. **Edmondson, A. C. (1999)** "Psychological safety and learning
   behavior." *ASQ*, 44(2). Upstream context: psychological safety in
   the conversation environment changes whether triggers fire at all.

3. **McAllister, D. J. (1995)** "Affect- and Cognition-Based Trust."
   *AMJ*, 38(1). The relationship trigger is partly a McAllister
   affective-trust failure -- low affective trust amplifies it.

## How citations are surfaced

- Every `AttachedPlaybook` carries an `anchor_citation` field.
- The system prompt anchors the LLM in Stone & Heen 2014 verbatim.
