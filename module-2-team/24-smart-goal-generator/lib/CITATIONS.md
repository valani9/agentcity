# SMART Goal Generator -- Citations

## Primary anchor

1. **Doran, G. T. (1981)** "There's a S.M.A.R.T. way to write
   management's goals and objectives." *Management Review*, 70(11),
   35-36. The five-criterion goal framework: Specific, Measurable,
   Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound.

## Supporting anchors

2. **Locke, E. A., & Latham, G. P. (2002)** "Building a practically
   useful theory of goal setting and task motivation." *American
   Psychologist*, 57(9). Modern goal-setting theory; informs the
   achievability calibration heuristics.

3. **Hackman, J. R. (2002)** *Leading Teams.* Source for the
   relevance criterion: goals must connect to the team's actual
   purpose, not vanity metrics.

## How citations are surfaced

- Every `AttachedPlaybook` carries an `anchor_citation` field.
- The system prompt names Doran 1981 verbatim.
