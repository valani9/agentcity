# Process Gain/Loss -- Literature Anchors

1. **Steiner, I. D. (1972)** *Group Process and Productivity.* Academic Press.
   The canonical process-loss framework. Six factors: coordination cost,
   social loafing, groupthink, handoff loss, context dilution, consensus
   dilution.

2. **Hill, G. W. (1982)** *Group versus Individual Performance: Are N + 1
   Heads Better than One?* Psychological Bulletin 91, 517-539. The
   empirical case for nominal-group aggregation.

3. **Hackman, J. R., & Vidmar, N. (1970)** *Effects of Size and Task Type
   on Group Performance and Member Reactions.* Sociometry 33, 37-54.
   Group-size effects.

4. **Diehl, M., & Stroebe, W. (1987)** *Productivity Loss in Brainstorming
   Groups: Toward the Solution of a Riddle.* JPSP 53, 497-509. The
   classical finding that brainstorming groups underperform nominal
   groups.

5. **Salas, E., et al. (2018)** *Science of Team Performance.* Annual
   Review of Org Psych 5, 593-620. Modern review.

6. **Robbins, S. P., & Judge, T. A.** *Organizational Behavior*. The
   workplace operationalization.

7. **Wang, X., et al. (2023)** Cooperative LLM Agents. Modern LLM
   multi-agent analog: orchestration patterns and their failure modes.

Plus Janis (1972) groupthink and Liu et al. (2024) lost-in-the-middle
cross-references.
