# Debate Pathology -- Citations

## Primary anchors

1. **Janis, I. L. (1972)** *Victims of Groupthink.* Houghton Mifflin.
   The groupthink construct + the eight symptoms (illusion of
   unanimity, self-censorship, etc.). Source for the groupthink-
   scoring rubric and the devil's advocate / round-robin dissent
   interventions.

2. **Stoner, J. A. F. (1968)** "Risky and cautious shifts in group
   decisions." *Journal of Experimental Social Psychology*, 4(4).
   Source for the group-polarization construct: the average post-
   discussion position is more extreme than the average pre-
   discussion position.

3. **Sunstein, C. R. (2002)** "The law of group polarization."
   *Journal of Political Philosophy*, 10(2). Modern operationalization
   of polarization + homogeneous-seeds failure mode.

4. **Hatfield, E., Cacioppo, J. T., & Rapson, R. L. (1993)**
   "Emotional contagion." *Current Directions in Psychological
   Science*, 2(3). Source for the contagion-scoring rubric and
   tone-normalization interventions.

## Supporting anchors

5. **Page, S. E. (2007)** *The Difference: How the Power of
   Diversity Creates Better Groups.* Princeton. Source for
   diverse_seed_positions intervention.

6. **Kahneman, D. (2011)** *Thinking, Fast and Slow.* FSG. Source
   for anchor_to_base_rates intervention.

## How citations are surfaced

- Every `AttachedPlaybook` carries an `anchor_citation` field.
- The system prompt names Janis 1972, Stoner 1968, Hatfield/Cacioppo/
  Rapson 1993 verbatim.
