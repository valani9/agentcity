# GRPI Working Agreement -- Literature Anchors

## Primary anchors

### 1. Beckhard, R. (1972)
> *Optimizing Team-Building Effort.* Journal of Contemporary Business 1, 23-32.

The canonical statement of the GRPI model: Goals, Roles, Processes,
Interactions. Beckhard's central claim is that team failure follows a
predictable order -- ambiguous goals cascade to ambiguous roles, which
cascade to broken processes, which cascade to dysfunctional interactions.
Working agreements must address all four dimensions explicitly.

### 2. Rubin, I. M., Plovnick, M. S., & Fry, R. E. (1977)
> *Task-Oriented Team Development.* McGraw-Hill.

The first formal operationalization of GRPI. Provides the assessment
instruments and intervention catalog. For AI agent systems the
operationalization is structural: working agreement as a system-prompt
artifact.

### 3. Hackman, J. R. (2002)
> *Leading Teams: Setting the Stage for Great Performances.* HBS Press.

The team-effectiveness anchor. Five conditions for great teams: real
team, compelling direction, enabling structure, supportive context,
expert coaching. GRPI is the "enabling structure" pillar.

### 4. Salas, E., et al. (2018)
> *The Science of Team Performance: Progress and the Need for Greater
> Integration.* Annual Review of Org Psych 5, 593-620.

Modern review. Maps team-performance science onto the GRPI dimensions
and provides the empirical basis for the intervention catalog.

### 5. Lencioni, P. (2002)
> *The Five Dysfunctions of a Team.* Jossey-Bass.

The inverse perspective: what a poor GRPI agreement enables. Five
dysfunctions: absence of trust, fear of conflict, lack of commitment,
avoidance of accountability, inattention to results. Forensic mode
explicitly audits the agreement against these five.

### 6. Edmondson, A. C. (1999)
> *Psychological Safety and Learning Behavior in Work Teams.*
> Administrative Science Quarterly, 44(2), 350-383.

The interactions-dimension anchor. Psychological safety predicts
team learning and performance independently of structural variables.
For agents: explicit psychological-safety commitments in the working
agreement.

### 7. Wang, X., et al. (2023) Cooperative LLM Agents

> Cooperative LLM Agents survey + modern orchestration frameworks
> (LangGraph, CrewAI, AutoGen, Anthropic Computer Use 2024).

The LLM-specific anchor. Modern multi-agent orchestration frameworks
have implicit GRPI structures; this pattern makes them explicit.
